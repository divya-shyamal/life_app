There are some apps I designed (and ChatGPT coded) to help me build healthy habits. You can download the html files for your own use! I was inspired after reading <a href="https://www.theatlantic.com/health/2026/05/monotasking-inside-the-box-excerpt-david-epstein/687015/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">this article from The Atlantic</a>. It explores the idea of maximizing productivity, and states that humans are not evolutionarily designed to stay focused on tasks for long periods of time. It also states that if you work for too long of a continuous time period, the chemical glutamine builds up in your brain, which is not good for you. Therefore, periods of focus time should be interrupted by breaks, where you do something enjoyable but not thought-heavy. Solving a puzzle, like Sudoku, is recommended.

This repo contains three apps:
<ul>
  <li>
    The first app is a simple focus timer, where you can input your tasks for the day, your estimated time for each, and what type of task it is. It will build a schedule for you, interleaving focus time and break time.
  </li>
  <li>
    The second app, Boggle, is my favorite puzzle. I decided to try and design my own version. This is what I do when I take a break from work!
  </li>
  <li>
    The third app is my favorite: it's a place to log the time you do work, the time you scroll, and your healthy habits. It encourages more of the good stuff and less of the bad stuff.
  </li>
</ul>
